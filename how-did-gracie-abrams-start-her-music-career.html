    How did Gracie Abrams start her music career?

    Gracie Madigan Abrams, the prodigious singer-songwriter born on September 7, 1999, has become a household name in the music industry, captivating audiences with her soulful melodies and heartfelt lyrics. Let’s delve into the intriguing story of how this Los Angeles native embarked on her musical odyssey.

    A Glimpse into Gracie’s Musical Palette

    Gracie Abrams, now signed to Interscope Records, boasts a repertoire that includes hits like “Mean It,” “I miss you, I’m sorry,” and “Feels Like.” With over 4.6 million monthly Spotify listeners and half a million followers on the platform, she has carved a niche for herself in the ever-evolving music landscape.

    Influences that Shaped a Sound

    The roots of Gracie’s musical prowess trace back to her early years, where she drew inspiration from legendary figures such as Joni Mitchell, Simon & Garfunkel, and Taylor Swift. Her eclectic taste spans across genres, encompassing influences from Bon Iver, Elvis Costello, Lorde, and Phoebe Bridgers.

    A Journey from YouTube Covers to Chart-Topping Singles

    Gracie kickstarted her musical journey by posting covers of various songs on YouTube. Her authentic renditions and undeniable talent caught the attention of the industry. In October 2019, she marked her official debut with the single “Mean It,” a poignant introduction to her soulful sound.

    EP Debut and Collaborations

    The release of her debut EP, “Minor,” on July 14, 2020, marked a significant milestone in Gracie’s career. The EP featured emotionally charged tracks like “I miss you, I’m sorry” and “Friend.” Her collaboration with Benny Blanco on “Unlearn” in March 2021 further showcased her versatility as an artist.

    A Flurry of Singles and Surprise Releases

    In 2021, Gracie continued to captivate audiences with singles like “Mess It Up” and “Feels Like,” the latter accompanied by a visually stunning music video. Collaborating with Aaron Dessner on “Rockland,” she displayed her ability to seamlessly blend her distinctive sound with different musical influences.

    “This Is What It Feels Like” – A Project of Artistic Liberation

    November 12, 2021, marked the release of Gracie’s much-anticipated project, “This Is What It Feels Like.” Featuring singles like “Alright” and “Rockland,” the project showcased her growth as an artist, blending vulnerability and strength in her lyrical narrative. The music video for “The Bottom” added another layer of visual storytelling to her repertoire.

    Touring with Olivia Rodrigo and Future Ventures

    Beyond the studio, Gracie Abrams took her talents on the road, opening for Olivia Rodrigo’s SOUR tour. The live performances allowed fans to experience the raw emotion and authenticity that defines Gracie’s music.

    Personal Life and Academic Interlude

    Raised in the artistic ambiance of Los Angeles, Gracie is the daughter of renowned film director J.J. Abrams and film and television producer Katie McGrath. Despite her familial ties to the entertainment industry, Gracie pursued higher education, briefly studying international relations at Barnard College after graduating from The Archer School for Girls in 2018.

    Discography and Future Promises

    Gracie’s discography, including standout releases like “Good Riddance” in 2023, showcases her evolving artistry. With a promising career ahead, she continues to be a force in the music industry, captivating audiences with her authentic storytelling and evocative melodies.

    As Gracie Abrams’ musical journey unfolds, fans eagerly anticipate the next chapter in the compelling narrative of this rising star.
