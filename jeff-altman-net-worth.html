    Jeff Altman Overview and Wiki

    Early Life and Rise to Fame

    Jeff Altman, born on August 13, 1951, in Syracuse, New York, embarked on his journey in the entertainment world after graduating from Johns Hopkins University with a degree in social sciences. His career kickstarted in 1974 at the renowned Comedy Store, marking the beginning of a journey filled with laughter and applause.

    Television and Film Career

    Altman’s television debut was in 1976 on Cos, a Bill Cosby children’s variety show. This was followed by a role in the Starland Vocal Band Show alongside celebrities like David Letterman and Mark Russell. In 1978, he appeared as Lennie Richfield in the movie American Hot Wax.

    Altman also hosted the short-lived NBC variety show Pink Lady and Jeff in 1980, which TV Guide ranked as one of the 50 Worst TV Shows of All Time.

    In the 1980s, Altman became the in-house comedian on Solid Gold, where he created memorable characters like Al Punker and Leonard Moon.

    He also directed many sitcoms during this period. Altman’s acting roles include Dr. Gene Splicer in Tiny Toon Adventures, Professor Dweeb in Slimer and The Real Ghostbusters, Clint Mullet on Mork and Mindy, and various characters on Baywatch. He had a recurring role as Hughie Hogg on The Dukes of Hazzard.

    Stand-up Comedy and Late Night Appearances

    A significant facet of Altman’s career is his stand-up comedy. His appearances on Late Night with David Letterman and Late Show with David Letterman a combined 45 times cemented his status as a comedy icon. His style, characterized by impressions and physical comedy, resonates with audiences even today.

    Other Ventures and Skills

    Altman’s talents extend beyond comedy and acting. He was the spokesman for Arby’s restaurants, Tostitos corn chips, Valvoline motor oil, and Budweiser. In 1982, he appeared in the movie Send in the Clowns.

    In 1989, Altman released his only comedy record album, I’ll Flip You Like A Cheese Omelette, and performed in various Hollywood clubs. He also hosted a defensive driving course for Blockbuster Video.

    In the 1990s, Altman was on the NBC series Nurses during its first season. He was known for his comical impression of his father and continued to find his Nixon impression humorous well into the 1980s.

    In 1991, he hosted The Sunday Comics and created his own show called Jeff Altman’s Scrap Book.

    In 2017, Altman played a fundraiser at the Chicago branch of the Laugh Factory for McCormac College of Lake View, Chicago.
