    Ron Kenoly Biography and Wiki

    Ron Kenoly is an American Christian worship leader, singer, and songwriter who was born on December 6, 1944, Coffeyville, Kansas, U.S. According to him, his expressed mission is “to create an environment for the manifest presence of God”.

    Kenoly’s musical style is one of jubilant praise and individual excellence in musical instruments. He leads comfortably with his voice and is always backed up by musicians and a large choir although he only plays on one of his recordings.

    Ron Kenoly Education

    Kenoly holds several degrees, including a music degree from Alameda College and a Master of Divinity from Faith Bible College. He is also a holder of a Doctorate of Ministry in Sacred music from Friends International Christian University.

    Music

    Kenoly ventured out to Hollywood, California after graduating from high school in his birthplace, Coffeyville, Kansas. In 1965, he joined the Air Force when plans to make it in the big city fell through. Ron met and married Tavita, his wife of 42 years, while in the military.

    It was also while there that the humble beginnings of his music career were launched. Kenoly joined the Mellow Fellows, a top 40 cover band that toured military bases. He left the Air Force and the Mellows Fellows behind in 1968 and shipped out to the City of Angels to go after his passion: music.

    Ron Kenoly Lift Him Up

    Kenoly began his music career shortly after leaving the United States Air Force. Originally, he was with a group called The Mellow Fellows, but family issues caused him to leave the group. In 1992, he had his critical breakthrough when Lift Him Up became the fastest-selling worship album to that point.

    His album Welcome Home, which was produced by Tom Brooks was also critically acclaimed, becoming Billboard’s Top contemporary worship music album. The album won Kenoly a Gospel Music Association Dove Award for “Praise and Worship Album” in 1997. Initially, he was signed to Integrity Music but he is no longer recording for them.
