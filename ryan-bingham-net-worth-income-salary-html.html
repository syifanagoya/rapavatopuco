    In 1981, George Ryan Bingham (born March 31, 1981) was born in Los Angeles, California, and is now a well-known Americana singer-songwriter. After receiving positive reviews for his first two studio albums on Lost Highway Records, 2007’s Mescalito and 2009’s Roadhouse Sun, Ryan Bingham went on to collaborate with Grammy-winning producer T Bone Burnett on the soundtrack for the critically acclaimed film Crazy Heart, most notably co-writing and performing the film’s award-winning theme song “The Weary Kind,” which won the Academy Award for Best Original Song. As a result of the title track’s success in 2010, Bingham was awarded an Academy Award for Best Song, as well as a Golden Globe Award and the Critics’ Choice Award. In 2011, Bingham was awarded a Grammy Award for “Best Song Written for a Motion Picture, Television, or Other Visual Media.”
